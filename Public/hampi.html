Hampi

Hampi, located in the Indian state of Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its rich history and stunning ruins. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi boasts a remarkable array of ancient temples, palaces, and monuments. Key attractions include the Virupaksha Temple, known for its intricate carvings, and the Lotus Mahal, with its unique architectural style. The ruins are scattered across a dramatic landscape of boulder-strewn hills and lush greenery. Hampi's historical and cultural significance, combined with its picturesque setting, makes it a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts and travelers alike.
